Jinhao Bilingual Education V.S International education

  Jinhe Elementary School is a school dedicated to cultivating students' bilingual proficiency and holistic development. We provide comprehensive bilingual education, enabling students to gain rich learning experiences in two languages.

  At Jinhe Elementary School, we value the cultivation of language abilities. Through rich language instruction and curriculum design, we ensure that students receive comprehensive development in listening, speaking, reading, and writing. Additionally, we emphasize cross-cultural exchange, allowing students to gain an understanding of cultures and values from around the world, fostering intercultural understanding and respect.

  In addition to language skills, we also focus on the development of the whole student. We offer a wide variety of extracurricular activities and clubs that allow students to develop their talents and interests in the arts, sports, and science. We focus on developing students' creativity, critical thinking, collaboration and problem-solving skills to meet the challenges of the future.

  The teaching team is the core resource at Jinhe Elementary School. We have professional, passionate, and culturally diverse teachers. They are dedicated to providing high-quality instruction, creating a positive learning environment, and working closely with parents to collectively nurture the students' learning and growth.

  At Jinhe Elementary School, we believe that every student possesses unlimited potential. Our mission is to inspire their creativity, nurture their talents, and prepare them to become outstanding global citizens. We are committed to providing quality education that cultivates students into confident, competent, and culturally literate individuals.

 

History

  Jinhe Elementary School was originally located in the area of NiuPu, which is now the neighborhoods of Jinhe and Jinchang. This was an ancient name during the early stages of development. In the 27th year of the Qing dynasty's Qianlong era (1762 AD), a man named Lin Tiansheng from Zhangzhou came to this area to cultivate the land. At that time, the land was covered with weeds and had not been farmed, making it suitable for raising cattle. It was named NiuPu after this. Later, due to the unsophisticated connotation of the name NiuPu, it was changed to Jinhe on September 1, 1972 (in the Republic of China era), and in 1986, JinChang was separated from Jinhe. The school was established in Jinhe, located on the border of Zhonghe District and Taipei City. It has been in operation for 35 years and is surrounded by a mix of old and new communities. However, the school and its surrounding natural environment are rich in cultural diversity, containing multiple elements such as the Zhanghe wetland, folk beliefs , and urban sentiments. The school has thus developed a locally sustainable curriculum with the theme of "Jinhe Cultural Youth." Its vision is to promote health, vitality, and creativity, and it has developed nine core competencies for character-building in young people, including "energetic," "super-powered," "international," "exploration," "leadership," "classic life," "wise life," "beautiful life," and "sustainable campus exploration." The school has designed related theme activities for each grade level, such as agricultural education, diverse clubs, health promotion, and sustainable campus exploration, to meet the learning needs of students. It aims to achieve its educational vision of "energetic innovation, caring warmth, respect, tolerance, harmony, and prosperity" through four major action plans, fostering the development of "world citizens who possess cooperative creativity, inquisitive thinking, caring action, and confident expression, capable of realizing their own achievements.

 

Strategy

Based on the "2030 New Taipei Bilingual City" educational vision set forth by the New Taipei City Government, our school implements the following specific strategies:

1. Integrated Learning: We incorporate English learning into daily life by dedicating one day per week as International Language Day. This includes bilingual broadcasting, bilingual conversations within the classroom, and encouraging teachers to conduct bilingual lessons. Additionally, we implement Content and Language Integrated Learning (CLIL) principles, utilizing the 4Cs (Content, Communication, Cognition, Culture) to convey concepts and texts, as well as facilitate interpersonal interactions, making bilingual learning more relevant to everyday life.

2. Technology-Enhanced Teaching: We encourage teachers to utilize teaching media such as tablets, touch screens, and other instructional technology. We also promote the use of online learning platforms for teachers, such as educational clouds, Learning Bar, InCARE, Cool Cloud, and K-12 Education Platform, to facilitate personalized learning and enhance students' independent learning capabilities through online learning processes.

3. Localized Specialization: We combine the development of school-based curriculum with the integration of local characteristics. By deeply cultivating our school-based curriculum and implementing cross-disciplinary integration, we aim to promote localized learning and specialization. This gradual shift in educational practices allows us to differentiate between bilingual education and international education. The following are further explanations:

a. Implementing School-based International Education: We have received subsidies from the Ministry of Education for school internationalization (SIEP-SI). This initiative involves deepening the development of curriculum and instruction within the school, planning international exchanges, and enhancing the substantive content of school internationalization.

b. Exploration Project for Sustainable Circular Campus: This project aims to lead teachers to venture outdoors and experience ecological and wetland curricula, continuously deepening environmental education and cross-disciplinary integration. It connects with the curriculum framework of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and aligns with international standards, enriching the distinctive curriculum.

c. Taiwan-US Eco-Partner Schools: We invite parents, teachers, students, and community members to participate in ecological conferences, where we collectively establish ecological declarations. With the concerted efforts of parents, teachers, and students, we strive to contribute to environmental education.

d. Four Lessons in International Education: We deepen the development and implementation of the Four Lessons in International Education curriculum. We actively participate in cross-school international education communities, engage in collaborative discussions on curriculum development, and integrate the curriculum into our school's English programs. These efforts effectively enhance our international perspectives and enrich the content of our curriculum.

e. International Exchange: In the 2020-2021 academic year, through the coordination of the Education Bureau, our school engaged in an exchange program with Yeoul Primary School in Seojong City, South Korea. Both schools discussed COVID-19 preventive measures and produced videos for mutual observation. As a gesture of goodwill, we exchanged commemorative gifts, with Yeoul Primary School receiving "little bags" from us and our school receiving masks from them. This exchange allowed students to experience different cultures and guided them in deepening their core competencies in international education.

4. Diversification of Modes: In line with the policies of the Education Bureau, we organize various activities, such as picture book creation, four-panel comics, English song contests, Reader's Theater (RT) competitions, English Speed Magic School holiday classes, and weekday classes.

5. Professional Development of Faculty: We continuously promote teachers' professional advancement by cultivating their secondary areas of expertise. We provide support for non-English subject teachers to adapt to bilingual instruction. Additionally, we encourage teachers to participate in professional development and workshops, fostering the enhancement of their specialized skills and optimizing the overall competence of both English and non-English subject teachers.
